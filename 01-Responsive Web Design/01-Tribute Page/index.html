Anne Frank

Jewish German Diarist & writer of the re‐knowned book The Diary of a Young Girl
Anne Frank's image on a hoarding at Amsterdam 8
  • First Years: Born in the German city of Frankfurt am Main in 1929. It was the period in which Adolf Hitler and his party were gaining more and more supporters. Hitler hated the Jews and blamed them for the problems in the country. The hatred of Jews and the poor economic situation made Anne's parents, decide to move to Amsterdam.
  • Neverthlands invaded by Nazi Germany: 1 September 1939, when Anne was 10 years old, Nazi Germany invaded Poland and Second World War began. 10 May 1940, the Nazis also invaded the Netherlands. Five days later, the Dutch army surrendered. The Nazis introduced more and more laws and regulations that made the lives of Jews more difficult. Anne's father lost his company, since Jews were no longer allowed to run their own businesses. All Jewish children, including Anne, had to go to separate Jewish schools.
  • Hiding in the Secret Annex: Margot, Anne's older sister, received a call-up to report for a so-called ‘labour camp’ in Nazi Germany on 5 July 1942, her parents were suspicious. They did not believe the call-up was about work and decided to go into hiding the next day in order to escape persecution. The hiding place was cramped. Anne had to keep very quiet and was often afraid.
  • Anne's Diary: On her thirteenth birthday, just before they went into hiding, Anne was presented with a diary. During the two years in hiding, Anne wrote about events in the Secret Annex, but also about her feelings and thoughts. Writing helped her pass the time. Her diary was later published titled "The Diary of a Young Girl".
  • The discovery of the hiding place: On 4 August 1944, she and the other people in hiding were discovered and arrested by police officers. Despite the raid, part of Anne’s writing was preserved.
  • Deported to Auschwitz: Via the offices of the Sicherheitsdienst (the German security police), a prison in Amsterdam, and the Westerbork transit camp, the people from the Secret Annex were put on transport to the Auschwitz-Birkenau concentration and extermination camp. Anne, Margot and their mother were sent to the labour camp for women. Otto( Anne's father ) ended up in a camp for men.
  • Anne dies from exhaustion in Bergen-Belsen: In early November 1944, Anne was put on transport again. She was deported to the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp with Margot. Their parents stayed behind in Auschwitz. The conditions in Bergen-Belsen were horrible. There was a lack of food, it was cold, wet and there were contagious diseases. Anne and Margot contracted typhus. In February 1945 they both died owing to its effects, Margot first, Anne shortly afterwards.
  • The Anne Frank House: People all over the world were introduced to Anne's story and in 1960 the hiding place became a museum: the Anne Frank House.
